What is the physical internet?

The internet is often imagined as weightless and immaterial, but every digital connection is anchored in a vast physical infrastructure.



Data travels through sprawling data centers, carried by underground transmission lines, powered by massive electrical facilities, and relayed across oceans through subsea cables that stretch for thousands of miles.



Cell towers punctuate skylines, translating signals between devices and networks, while server farms hum quietly on the outskirts of cities, consuming enormous amounts of energy to keep information flowing.



This physical internet reveals that our digital world is not detached from the material one, it is deeply rooted in landscapes of steel, concrete, and wire.
